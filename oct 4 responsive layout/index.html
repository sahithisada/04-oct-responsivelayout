Violet

Wavelength:

380–425 nm

Symbolism:

Violet symbolizes creativity, mystery, and spirituality, often associated with luxury and nobility in various cultures.

Nature:

It is found in beautiful flowers like violets and lavender, as well as in the hues of twilight, embodying elegance and the transition between day and night.

Indigo

Wavelength:

425–450 nm

Symbolism:

Indigo is often seen as a mystical color, representing intuition, wisdom, and spirituality, blending the calmness of blue with the depth of violet.

Nature:

Indigo can be found in some plants, such as indigofera, which was historically used for dye, reflecting the color’s rich cultural and historical significance.

Blue

Wavelength:

450–495 nm.

Symbolism:

Blue represents calmness, stability, and trust, often evoking feelings of serenity and peace in various settings.

Nature:

It is found in the expansive sky and vast bodies of water, such as oceans and lakes, symbolizing depth and tranquility in the natural world.

Green

Wavelength:

495–570 nm.

Symbolism:

Green symbolizes growth, renewal, and harmony, representing balance in nature and often associated with fertility and life.

Nature:

It is the dominant color in lush plants, trees, and grasslands, reflecting health and sustainability in ecosystems, and is vital for photosynthesis.

Yellow

Wavelength:

570–590 nm.

Symbolism:

Yellow represents happiness, positivity, and clarity, often bringing feelings of cheerfulness and optimism to those who see it.

Nature:

It is seen in sunny flowers like daffodils and sunflowers, as well as in bright fruits such as bananas and lemons, embodying the essence of sunlight and joy.

Orange

Wavelength:

590–620 nm.

Symbolism:

Orange signifies enthusiasm, creativity, and warmth, often associated with joy and the vibrant energy of life.

Nature:

It appears prominently in autumn leaves, signaling change, and in ripe fruits like oranges and pumpkins, representing harvest and abundance.

Red

Wavelength:

620-750 nm.

Symbolism:

Red represents love, passion, and energy, often evoking strong emotions and urgency in various cultural contexts.

Nature:

It is commonly found in vibrant flowers, such as roses and poppies, and in natural phenomena like stunning sunsets, symbolizing vitality and strength.
